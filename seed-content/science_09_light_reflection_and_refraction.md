# Light - Reflection and Refraction - Learning Path

## 1. Concept Introduction

### What is Light?

**Light** is a form of energy that produces the sensation of vision in our eyes. It travels in straight lines and does not require a medium for propagation.

**Everyday Examples:**
- 🪞 **Mirrors:** Seeing our reflection
- 🌈 **Rainbow:** Colorful arc after rain
- 🔍 **Magnifying glass:** Making objects appear larger
- 📷 **Cameras:** Capturing images
- 👓 **Spectacles:** Correcting vision

## 3. Concept Explanations

### 9.1 Reflection of Light

**Reflection:** Bouncing back of light when it strikes a smooth surface.

#### Laws of Reflection:

```
LAWS OF REFLECTION

    Incident Ray    Normal    Reflected Ray
         \            |            /
          \           |           /
           \    i     |     r    /
            \         |         /
             \        |        /
              \       |       /
               \      |      /
                \     |     /
                 \    |    /
                  \   |   /
                   \  |  /
                    \ | /
                     \|/
    ──────────────────┴──────────────────
                    Mirror
    
    Law 1: ∠i = ∠r
    (Angle of incidence = Angle of reflection)
    
    Law 2: Incident ray, reflected ray,
           and normal lie in same plane
```

**Terms:**
- **Incident Ray:** Ray striking the surface
- **Reflected Ray:** Ray bouncing back
- **Normal:** Perpendicular to surface
- **Angle of Incidence (i):** Between incident ray and normal
- **Angle of Reflection (r):** Between reflected ray and normal

---

#### Spherical Mirrors:

**Types:**

```
SPHERICAL MIRRORS

    Concave Mirror          Convex Mirror
    (Converging)            (Diverging)
    
         ╱│╲                     │
        ╱ │ ╲                   ╱│╲
       ╱  │  ╲                 ╱ │ ╲
      ╱   │   ╲               ╱  │  ╲
     ╱    │    ╲             ╱   │   ╲
    ╱     │     ╲           ╱    │    ╲
   ╱      │      ╲         ╱     │     ╲
  ────────┴───────       ────────┴───────
  
  Reflecting surface      Reflecting surface
  curved inward           curved outward
  
  Can form real/virtual   Always forms virtual
  Can be magnified        Always diminished
```

**Terms Related to Spherical Mirrors:**

| Term | Definition | Symbol |
|------|------------|--------|
| **Pole** | Center of mirror surface | P |
| **Center of Curvature** | Center of sphere | C |
| **Radius of Curvature** | Radius of sphere | R |
| **Principal Axis** | Line through P and C | - |
| **Focus** | Point where parallel rays meet | F |
| **Focal Length** | Distance between P and F | f |

**Relationship:**
$$ R = 2f $$
$$ f = \frac{R}{2} $$

---

#### Image Formation by Spherical Mirrors:

**Concave Mirror:**

```
IMAGE FORMATION BY CONCAVE MIRROR

Position of Object    Position of Image    Nature    Size
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
At infinity          At F                Real      Highly
                                            diminished
Beyond C             Between F and C     Real      Diminished
At C                 At C                Real      Same size
Between C and F      Beyond C            Real      Magnified
At F                 At infinity         Real      Highly
                                            magnified
Between F and P      Behind mirror       Virtual   Magnified
```

**Convex Mirror:**

```
IMAGE FORMATION BY CONVEX MIRROR

Position of Object    Position of Image    Nature    Size
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
At infinity          At F (behind)       Virtual   Highly
                                            diminished
Between infinity     Between P and F     Virtual   Diminished
and P                (behind mirror)
```

**Uses:**
- **Concave:** Torches, headlights, shaving mirrors, dentist mirrors
- **Convex:** Rear-view mirrors, street lights

---

#### Mirror Formula:

$$ \frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f} $$

Where:
- **u** = Object distance (from pole)
- **v** = Image distance (from pole)
- **f** = Focal length

**Magnification:**

$$ m = \frac{h'}{h} = -\frac{v}{u} $$

Where:
- **h'** = Height of image
- **h** = Height of object

**Sign Convention:**
- All distances measured from pole
- Direction of incident light: Positive
- Opposite direction: Negative
- Above principal axis: Positive
- Below principal axis: Negative

### 9.2 Refraction of Light

**Refraction:** Bending of light when it passes from one medium to another.

#### Laws of Refraction:

```
LAWS OF REFRACTION

    Medium 1 (Rarer)
         \            Normal
          \           |
           \    i     |
            \         |
             \        |
              \       |
    ────────────\─────|────────────── Interface
                \     |
                 \    |
                  \   |
                   \  |
                    \ |
                     \|
                      \
                       \    r
                        \
    Medium 2 (Denser)    \
    
    Law 1: Incident ray, refracted ray,
           and normal lie in same plane
    
    Law 2: Snell's Law
           sin i / sin r = constant (n)
```

**Refractive Index:**

$$ n = \frac{\sin i}{\sin r} = \frac{c}{v} $$

Where:
- **n** = Refractive index
- **c** = Speed of light in vacuum (3 × 10⁸ m/s)
- **v** = Speed of light in medium

**Refractive Indices of Common Media:**

| Medium | Refractive Index |
|--------|-----------------|
| Air | 1.0003 |
| Water | 1.33 |
| Kerosene | 1.44 |
| Glass | 1.50 - 1.65 |
| Diamond | 2.42 |

```
    (Converging)             (Diverging)
    
         │                         │
        ╱│╲                       ╱│╲
       ╱ │ ╲                     ╱ │ ╲
      ╱  │  ╲                   ╱  │  ╲
```

#### Image Formation by Lenses:

**Convex Lens:**

```
IMAGE FORMATION BY CONVEX LENS

Position of Object    Position of Image    Nature    Size
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
At infinity          At F₂               Real      Highly
                                            diminished
Beyond 2F₁           Between F₂ and 2F₂  Real      Diminished
At 2F₁               At 2F₂              Real      Same size
Between F₁ and 2F₁   Beyond 2F₂          Real      Magnified
At F₁                At infinity         Real      Highly
                                            magnified
Between F₁ and O     On same side        Virtual   Magnified
```

**Concave Lens:**

```
IMAGE FORMATION BY CONCAVE LENS

Position of Object    Position of Image    Nature    Size
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
At infinity          At F₁ (same side)   Virtual   Highly
                                            diminished
Between infinity     Between F₁ and O    Virtual   Diminished
and O                (same side)
```

**Uses:**
- **Convex:** Magnifying glass, cameras, telescopes, correcting hypermetropia
- **Concave:** Correcting myopia, peepholes

---

#### Lens Formula:

$$ \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f} $$

Where:
- **u** = Object distance (from optical center)
- **v** = Image distance (from optical center)
- **f** = Focal length

**Magnification:**

$$ m = \frac{h'}{h} = \frac{v}{u} $$

**Sign Convention for Lenses:**
- All distances measured from optical center
- Direction of incident light: Positive
- Opposite direction: Negative
- Above principal axis: Positive
- Below principal axis: Negative

---

### 9.3 Power of Lens

**Power:** Ability of lens to converge or diverge light.

$$ P = \frac{1}{f} $$

Where:
- **P** = Power of lens (in Dioptres, D)
- **f** = Focal length (in meters)

**Sign:**
- **Convex lens:** Positive power
- **Concave lens:** Negative power

**Combination of Lenses:**

$$ P_{total} = P_1 + P_2 + P_3 + ... $$

**Example:**
- Lens 1: +2D (convex)
- Lens 2: -1D (concave)
- Total Power: +2 + (-1) = +1D

## 4. Guided Examples

### Example 1: Mirror Formula Application

**Question:** An object is placed 20 cm in front of a concave mirror of focal length 15 cm. Find the position and nature of the image.

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

| Step | Action | Working |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Identify given values | u = -20 cm, f = -15 cm |
| 2 | Apply mirror formula | $\frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$ |
| 3 | Substitute values | $\frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{-20} = \frac{1}{-15}$ |
| 4 | Solve for v | $\frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{-15} - \frac{1}{-20}$ |
| | | $\frac{1}{v} = \frac{-4+3}{60} = \frac{-1}{60}$ |
| 5 | Find v | v = -60 cm |
| 6 | Determine nature | Negative v = Real image |
| | | Image is in front of mirror |

**Final Answer:** Image is formed 60 cm in front of mirror, real and inverted.

---

### Example 2: Magnification Calculation

**Question:** An object of height 5 cm is placed 30 cm in front of a concave mirror. The image is formed 60 cm from the mirror. Find the height of the image.

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

| Step | Action | Working |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Identify given values | h = 5 cm, u = -30 cm, v = -60 cm |
| 2 | Apply magnification formula | $m = -\frac{v}{u} = \frac{h'}{h}$ |
| 3 | Calculate m | $m = -\frac{-60}{-30} = -2$ |
| 4 | Find h' | $h' = m \times h = -2 \times 5 = -10$ cm |
| 5 | Interpret sign | Negative = Inverted image |

**Final Answer:** Height of image = 10 cm, inverted.

---

### Example 3: Refractive Index Calculation

**Question:** Speed of light in a medium is 2 × 10⁸ m/s. Calculate its refractive index. (Speed of light in vacuum = 3 × 10⁸ m/s)

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

| Step | Action | Working |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Identify given values | v = 2 × 10⁸ m/s, c = 3 × 10⁸ m/s |
| 2 | Apply refractive index formula | $n = \frac{c}{v}$ |
| 3 | Substitute values | $n = \frac{3 \times 10^8}{2 \times 10^8}$ |
| 4 | Calculate | $n = 1.5$ |

**Final Answer:** Refractive index = 1.5

---

### Example 4: Lens Formula Application

**Question:** An object is placed 25 cm from a convex lens of focal length 10 cm. Find the position and nature of the image.

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

| Step | Action | Working |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Identify given values | u = -25 cm, f = +10 cm |
| 2 | Apply lens formula | $\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f}$ |
| 3 | Substitute values | $\frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{-25} = \frac{1}{10}$ |
| 4 | Solve for v | $\frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{10} - \frac{1}{25}$ |
| | | $\frac{1}{v} = \frac{5-2}{50} = \frac{3}{50}$ |
| 5 | Find v | v = 16.67 cm |
| 6 | Determine nature | Positive v = Real image |
| | | Image is on opposite side |

**Final Answer:** Image is formed 16.67 cm on opposite side, real and inverted.

---

### Example 5: Power of Lens Combination

**Question:** Two lenses of power +3D and -2D are placed in contact. Find the focal length of the combination.

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

| Step | Action | Working |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Identify given values | P₁ = +3D, P₂ = -2D |
| 2 | Find total power | P = P₁ + P₂ = +3 + (-2) = +1D |
| 3 | Apply power formula | $P = \frac{1}{f}$ |
| 4 | Solve for f | $f = \frac{1}{P} = \frac{1}{1} = 1$ m |
| 5 | Interpret sign | Positive = Convex lens |

**Final Answer:** Focal length = 1 m (100 cm), convex lens.

```
    Using sign convention:
    h/(-h') = (-u)/v = f/(v-f)
    
    After simplification:
    1/v - 1/u = 1/f
```

**Lens Formula:**
$$ \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f} $$

**(b) Sign Convention for Lenses:**

**New Cartesian Sign Convention:**

1. **Origin:** Optical center (O)

2. **Direction:**
   - Incident light direction: Positive (+)
   - Opposite direction: Negative (-)

3. **Distances:**
   - Measured from optical center
   - Right of O: Positive
   - Left of O: Negative

4. **Heights:**
   - Above principal axis: Positive
   - Below principal axis: Negative

5. **Focal Length:**
   - Convex lens: Positive
   - Concave lens: Negative
</details>

**Q63.** (a) An object is placed 30 cm from a concave mirror of focal length 15 cm. Find the position, nature and size of image if object height is 5 cm.
(b) Draw the ray diagram.

<details>
<summary>✓ Show Answer</summary>

**Answer:**

**(a) Calculation:**

**Given:**
- u = -30 cm
- f = -15 cm
- h = 5 cm

**Mirror Formula:**
$$ \frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u} = \frac{1}{f} $$
$$ \frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{-30} = \frac{1}{-15} $$
$$ \frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{-15} - \frac{1}{-30} $$
$$ \frac{1}{v} = \frac{-2+1}{30} = \frac{-1}{30} $$

**v = -30 cm**

**Position:** 30 cm in front of mirror

**Magnification:**
$$ m = -\frac{v}{u} = -\frac{-30}{-30} = -1 $$

**Height of image:**
$$ h' = m \times h = -1 \times 5 = -5 \text{ cm} $$

**Nature:**
- Real (v is negative)
- Inverted (h' is negative)
- Same size (|m| = 1)

**Answer:** Image at 30 cm in front, real, inverted, same size (5 cm)

**(b) Ray Diagram:**

**Q65.** (a) Explain the formation of rainbow.
(b) Why is rainbow always opposite to the sun?
(c) What is the order of colors in primary and secondary rainbow?

<details>
<summary>✓ Show Answer</summary>

**Answer:**

**(a) Formation of Rainbow:**

**Process:**

```
    Sunlight
       │
       │
       ▼
    ┌─────┐
    │Water│
    │Drop │
    └─────┘
       │
    Refraction (dispersion)
       │
    Internal reflection
       │
    Refraction again
       │
       ▼
    Observer sees rainbow
    
    Each drop acts as prism
    Millions of drops form arc
```

**Steps:**

1. **Refraction:** Sunlight enters water droplet
2. **Dispersion:** Splits into colors
3. **Internal Reflection:** Reflects inside droplet
4. **Refraction:** Exits droplet, bends again
5. **Observer:** Sees colored arc

**(b) Opposite to Sun:**

**Reason:**

1. **Light path:**
   - Enters droplet from sun side
   - Reflects internally
   - Exits toward observer

2. **Geometry:**
   - Angle of deviation: 42° (red) to 40° (violet)
   - Observer must be between sun and rainbow
   - Rainbow forms opposite arc

3. **Position:**
   - Sun behind observer
   - Rainbow in front
   - Always opposite

**(c) Color Order:**

**Primary Rainbow:**
- **Outer:** Red (42°)
- **Inner:** Violet (40°)
- **Order:** VIBGYOR (inside to outside)
- **Brightness:** Brighter
- **Formation:** One internal reflection

**Secondary Rainbow:**
- **Outer:** Violet (50°)
- **Inner:** Red (53°)
- **Order:** ROYGBIV (inside to outside)
- **Brightness:** Fainter
- **Formation:** Two internal reflections
- **Position:** Outside primary rainbow
</details>
