# Chemical Reactions and Equations - Learning Path

## 1. Concept Introduction

### What is a Chemical Reaction?

A **chemical reaction** is a process in which one or more substances (called **reactants**) are converted into new substances (called **products**) with different chemical properties.

**Everyday Examples:**
- 🍞 Bread turning brown when toasted
- 🥛 Milk becoming sour on a hot day
- 🔥 Burning of wood or paper
- 🧊 Rusting of iron nails
- 🎆 Fireworks exploding in the sky

### How Do We Know a Chemical Reaction Has Occurred?

Look for these **observable changes**:

| Observation | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| Change in state | Ice melting to water |
| Change in colour | Iron turning reddish-brown (rust) |
| Evolution of gas | Bubbles when vinegar mixes with baking soda |
| Change in temperature | Heat released when burning fuel |
| Formation of precipitate | White solid when mixing silver nitrate and sodium chloride |

## 3. Concept Explanations

### 3.1 Chemical Equations

A **chemical equation** is a symbolic representation of a chemical reaction using chemical formulas and symbols.

**Example:**

Reactants → Products

Magnesium + Oxygen → Magnesium Oxide

$$ 2\text{Mg} + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{MgO} $$

#### Parts of a Chemical Equation:

- **Reactants:** Substances that react (left side)
- **Products:** Substances formed (right side)
- **Arrow (→):** Direction of reaction
- **State symbols:** (s) solid, (l) liquid, (g) gas, (aq) aqueous solution

**Complete Example:**

$$ 2\text{Mg}_{(s)} + \text{O}_{2(g)} \xrightarrow{\text{heat}} 2\text{MgO}_{(s)} $$

#### Balanced vs Unbalanced Equations

**Unbalanced Equation:**

$$ \text{H}_2 + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow \text{H}_2\text{O} $$

❌ Not balanced: 2 oxygen atoms on left, 1 on right

**Balanced Equation:**

$$ 2\text{H}_2 + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{H}_2\text{O} $$

✅ Balanced: 4 H and 2 O atoms on both sides

**Why Balance Equations?**
According to the **Law of Conservation of Mass**:

> Matter can neither be created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction.

---

### 3.2 Types of Chemical Reactions

#### Type 1: Combination Reaction

Two or more reactants combine to form a **single product**.

**General Form:**

$$ \text{A} + \text{B} \rightarrow \text{AB} $$

**Examples:**

1. Burning of magnesium ribbon:

$$ 2\text{Mg}_{(s)} + \text{O}_{2(g)} \rightarrow 2\text{MgO}_{(s)} $$

2. Formation of water:

$$ 2\text{H}_{2(g)} + \text{O}_{2(g)} \rightarrow 2\text{H}_2\text{O}_{(l)} $$

3. Quick lime + Water:

$$ \text{CaO}_{(s)} + \text{H}_2\text{O}_{(l)} \rightarrow \text{Ca(OH)}_{2(aq)} $$

---

#### Type 2: Decomposition Reaction

A **single compound** breaks down into two or more simpler substances.

**General Form:**

$$ \text{AB} \rightarrow \text{A} + \text{B} $$

**Types of Decomposition:**

| Type | Energy Source | Example |
|------|---------------|---------|
| Thermal | Heat | $$ \text{CaCO}_{3(s)} \xrightarrow{\Delta} \text{CaO}_{(s)} + \text{CO}_{2(g)} $$ |
| Electrolytic | Electricity | $$ 2\text{H}_2\text{O}_{(l)} \xrightarrow{\text{electricity}} 2\text{H}_{2(g)} + \text{O}_{2(g)} $$ |
| Photochemical | Light | $$ 2\text{AgBr}_{(s)} \xrightarrow{\text{sunlight}} 2\text{Ag}_{(s)} + \text{Br}_{2(g)} $$ |

**Real-World Application:**
- Photography uses silver bromide decomposition in sunlight

---

#### Type 3: Displacement Reaction

A **more reactive element** displaces a less reactive element from its compound.

**General Form:**

$$ \text{A} + \text{BC} \rightarrow \text{AC} + \text{B} $$

**Example:**

$$ \text{Fe}_{(s)} + \text{CuSO}_{4(aq)} \rightarrow \text{FeSO}_{4(aq)} + \text{Cu}_{(s)} $$

**Observations:**
- Iron nail turns brown
- Blue colour of copper sulphate fades

**Reactivity Series (Most to Least Reactive):**

```
K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Pb > H > Cu > Ag > Au
```

---

#### Type 4: Double Displacement Reaction

Exchange of ions between two compounds to form **two new compounds**.

**General Form:**

$$ \text{AB} + \text{CD} \rightarrow \text{AD} + \text{CB} $$

**Example:**

$$ \text{Na}_2\text{SO}_{4(aq)} + \text{BaCl}_{2(aq)} \rightarrow \text{BaSO}_{4(s)} \downarrow + 2\text{NaCl}_{(aq)} $$

**White precipitate** of barium sulphate is formed.

---

### 3.3 Oxidation and Reduction (Redox Reactions)

#### Oxidation
- Addition of **oxygen** or removal of **hydrogen**
- Loss of **electrons**

**Example:**

$$ 2\text{Cu}_{(s)} + \text{O}_{2(g)} \xrightarrow{\text{heat}} 2\text{CuO}_{(s)} $$

(Copper is oxidized)

#### Reduction
- Removal of **oxygen** or addition of **hydrogen**
- Gain of **electrons**

**Example:**

$$ \text{CuO}_{(s)} + \text{H}_{2(g)} \xrightarrow{\text{heat}} \text{Cu}_{(s)} + \text{H}_2\text{O}_{(l)} $$

(Copper oxide is reduced)

#### Redox Reaction
Both oxidation and reduction occur **simultaneously**.

**Example:**

$$ \text{ZnO}_{(s)} + \text{C}_{(s)} \rightarrow \text{Zn}_{(s)} + \text{CO}_{(g)} $$

| Substance | Change | Process |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| ZnO → Zn | Loss of oxygen | Reduction |
| C → CO | Gain of oxygen | Oxidation |

---

### 3.4 Effects of Oxidation in Everyday Life

#### Corrosion (Rusting of Iron)

**What happens?**
Iron reacts with oxygen and moisture to form hydrated iron(III) oxide (rust).

**Chemical Equation:**

$$ 4\text{Fe}_{(s)} + 3\text{O}_{2(g)} + 2x\text{H}_2\text{O}_{(l)} \rightarrow 2\text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3 \cdot x\text{H}_2\text{O}_{(s)} $$

**Prevention Methods:**
- Painting or greasing
- Galvanization (coating with zinc)
- Electroplating
- Making alloys (stainless steel)

---

#### Rancidity (Spoilage of Food)

**What happens?**
Fats and oils in food get oxidized, producing unpleasant smell and taste.

**Prevention Methods:**
- Adding antioxidants (BHT, BHA)
- Packaging in nitrogen gas
- Refrigeration
- Storing in airtight containers
- Keeping away from sunlight

## 4. Guided Examples

### Example 1: Balancing a Chemical Equation

**Question:** Balance the following equation:

$$ \text{Fe} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{Fe}_3\text{O}_4 + \text{H}_2 $$

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

| Step | Action | Equation |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Count atoms on each side | Fe: 1, H: 2, O: 1 (left) vs Fe: 3, H: 2, O: 4 (right) |
| 2 | Balance Fe (multiply by 3) | $$ 3\text{Fe} + \text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{Fe}_3\text{O}_4 + \text{H}_2 $$ |
| 3 | Balance O (multiply H₂O by 4) | $$ 3\text{Fe} + 4\text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{Fe}_3\text{O}_4 + \text{H}_2 $$ |
| 4 | Balance H (multiply H₂ by 4) | $$ 3\text{Fe} + 4\text{H}_2\text{O} \rightarrow \text{Fe}_3\text{O}_4 + 4\text{H}_2 $$ |
| 5 | Verify | ✅ Fe: 3, H: 8, O: 4 on both sides |

**Final Answer:**

$$ 3\text{Fe}_{(s)} + 4\text{H}_2\text{O}_{(g)} \rightarrow \text{Fe}_3\text{O}_{4(s)} + 4\text{H}_{2(g)} $$

---

### Example 2: Identifying Reaction Type

**Question:** Identify the type of reaction:

$$ 2\text{Pb(NO}_3\text{)}_{2(s)} \xrightarrow{\text{heat}} 2\text{PbO}_{(s)} + 4\text{NO}_{2(g)} + \text{O}_{2(g)} $$

**Solution:**

1. **Observe:** One reactant → Multiple products
2. **Energy:** Heat is applied (Δ)
3. **Conclusion:** This is a **thermal decomposition reaction**

---

### Example 3: Displacement Reaction Prediction

**Question:** Will this reaction occur?

$$ \text{Cu}_{(s)} + \text{FeSO}_{4(aq)} \rightarrow \text{?} $$

**Solution:**

1. Check reactivity series: Fe is **more reactive** than Cu
2. Copper **cannot** displace iron from its salt solution
3. **Answer:** No reaction will occur

**Q10 (CBSE 2019):** Explain double displacement reaction with example. Is it different from displacement reaction?

**Answer:**

**Double Displacement:** Exchange of ions between two compounds.

Example:
$$ \text{AgNO}_{3(aq)} + \text{NaCl}_{(aq)} \rightarrow \text{AgCl}_{(s)} + \text{NaNO}_{3(aq)} $$

**Different from displacement:**
- Displacement: One element replaces another
- Double displacement: Ions exchange between compounds

| Displacement | Double Displacement |
|--------------|---------------------|
| $$ \text{Zn} + \text{CuSO}_4 \rightarrow \text{ZnSO}_4 + \text{Cu} $$ | $$ \text{AgNO}_3 + \text{NaCl} \rightarrow \text{AgCl} + \text{NaNO}_3 $$ |

#### Types of Reactions (Summary):

**1. COMBINATION:** Two or more reactants form single product

$$ \text{A} + \text{B} \rightarrow \text{AB} $$

Example: $$ 2\text{H}_2 + \text{O}_2 \rightarrow 2\text{H}_2\text{O} $$

**2. DECOMPOSITION:** Single compound breaks into simpler substances

$$ \text{AB} \rightarrow \text{A} + \text{B} $$

Example: $$ \text{CaCO}_3 \xrightarrow{\Delta} \text{CaO} + \text{CO}_2 $$

**3. DISPLACEMENT:** More reactive element displaces less reactive

$$ \text{A} + \text{BC} \rightarrow \text{AC} + \text{B} $$

Example: $$ \text{Zn} + \text{CuSO}_4 \rightarrow \text{ZnSO}_4 + \text{Cu} $$

**4. DOUBLE DISPLACEMENT:** Exchange of ions between compounds

$$ \text{AB} + \text{CD} \rightarrow \text{AD} + \text{CB} $$

Example: $$ \text{AgNO}_3 + \text{NaCl} \rightarrow \text{AgCl} + \text{NaNO}_3 $$

#### Important Equations (Must Remember):

**1. Magnesium burning:**
$$ 2\text{Mg}_{(s)} + \text{O}_{2(g)} \rightarrow 2\text{MgO}_{(s)} $$

**2. Electrolysis of water:**
$$ 2\text{H}_2\text{O}_{(l)} \xrightarrow{\text{electricity}} 2\text{H}_{2(g)} + \text{O}_{2(g)} $$

**3. Iron + Copper sulphate:**
$$ \text{Fe}_{(s)} + \text{CuSO}_{4(aq)} \rightarrow \text{FeSO}_{4(aq)} + \text{Cu}_{(s)} $$

**4. Thermal decomposition:**
$$ \text{CaCO}_{3(s)} \xrightarrow{\Delta} \text{CaO}_{(s)} + \text{CO}_{2(g)} $$

**5. Double displacement:**
$$ \text{Na}_2\text{SO}_{4(aq)} + \text{BaCl}_{2(aq)} \rightarrow \text{BaSO}_{4(s)} \downarrow + 2\text{NaCl}_{(aq)} $$

**6. Photochemical (Photography):**
$$ 2\text{AgBr}_{(s)} \xrightarrow{\text{sunlight}} 2\text{Ag}_{(s)} + \text{Br}_{2(g)} $$

**7. Respiration:**
$$ \text{C}_6\text{H}_{12}\text{O}_{6(aq)} + 6\text{O}_{2(g)} \rightarrow 6\text{CO}_{2(g)} + 6\text{H}_2\text{O}_{(l)} + \text{Energy} $$

**8. Rusting:**
$$ 4\text{Fe}_{(s)} + 3\text{O}_{2(g)} + 2x\text{H}_2\text{O}_{(l)} \rightarrow 2\text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3 \cdot x\text{H}_2\text{O}_{(s)} $$

**9. Quick lime + Water:**
$$ \text{CaO}_{(s)} + \text{H}_2\text{O}_{(l)} \rightarrow \text{Ca(OH)}_{2(aq)} + \text{Heat} $$

**10. Lead nitrate heating:**
$$ 2\text{Pb(NO}_3\text{)}_{2(s)} \xrightarrow{\Delta} 2\text{PbO}_{(s)} + 4\text{NO}_{2(g)} + \text{O}_{2(g)} $$
