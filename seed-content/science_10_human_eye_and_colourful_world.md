- 🌈 **Rainbow:** Colorful arc after rainfall
- 🔭 **Telescope:** Viewing distant stars and planets

### 10.1 The Human Eye

**The Human Eye** is a natural optical instrument that forms real and inverted images on the retina.

#### Structure of Human Eye:

#### Parts and Functions:

| Part | Description | Function |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Cornea** | Transparent front membrane | Initial refraction of light |
| **Iris** | Colored part of eye | Controls pupil size |
| **Pupil** | Black opening in iris | Allows light entry |
| **Eye Lens** | Convex, flexible lens | Focuses light on retina |
| **Ciliary Muscles** | Muscles holding lens | Adjust lens curvature |
| **Retina** | Light-sensitive screen | Contains rods and cones |
| **Optic Nerve** | Nerve bundle | Carries signals to brain |
| **Aqueous Humour** | Watery fluid | Maintains shape, nourishes |
| **Vitreous Humour** | Jelly-like substance | Maintains shape |

#### Power of Accommodation:

**Definition:** Ability of eye lens to change its focal length to focus on objects at different distances.

```
ACCOMMODATION MECHANISM

    For Distant Objects:
    ────────────────────
    Ciliary muscles: RELAXED
    Lens: THIN
    Focal length: LONG
    Light rays: Less bent
    
         ═══════════
         Thin Lens
         ═══════════
    
    For Near Objects:
    ─────────────────
    Ciliary muscles: CONTRACTED
    Lens: THICK
    Focal length: SHORT
    Light rays: More bent
    
          ═══
         ═ Lens ═
          ═══
          Thick
```

**Importance:**
- Clear vision at all distances
- Automatic adjustment
- Reduces eye strain
- Lost in presbyopia (old age)

---

#### Persistence of Vision:

**Definition:** Retina retains image for 1/16th second after object is removed.

**Applications:**

1. **Cinema:**
   - 24 frames per second
   - Each frame persists
   - Continuous motion perceived

2. **Television:**
   - Scanning creates images
   - Persistence blends them
   - Smooth picture

3. **Flip Books:**
   - Series of images
   - Quick flipping
   - Animation effect

4. **Rotating Wheel:**
   - Spokes appear blurred
   - Individual spokes not visible
   - Continuous disc appears

### 10.2 Defects of Vision

#### 1. Myopia (Near-sightedness):

**Definition:** Inability to see distant objects clearly.

```
MYOPIA (Near-sightedness)

    Distant Object
         │
         │
    ─────┼─────────────────────────────
         │      │
         │      │
         │      │
    ─────┼──────┼─────────────────────
         │      │
         │      │
         │      │
         │      │
    ─────┼──────┼─────────────────────
        Lens   Retina
               │
               │
            Image (in front of retina)
            Blurred vision
    
    Causes:
    - Eyeball too long
    - Lens too curved
    - Focal length too short
    
    Far point: Less than infinity
    Near point: Normal (25 cm)
```

**Correction:**
- **Concave lens** (diverging)
- Diverges light before entering eye
- Image forms on retina

```
CORRECTION OF MYOPIA

         Concave Lens
         (diverges)
             │
             │
    ─────────┼─────────────────────────
             │
             │
             │
    ─────────┼──────┼─────────────────
             │      │
             │      │
             │      │
             │      │
    ─────────┼──────┼─────────────────
            Lens   Retina
                   │
                   │
                Image (on retina)
                Clear vision
```

**Power of Lens:**
$$ P = -\frac{1}{f} $$
where f = distance of far point (in meters)

#### 3. Presbyopia:

**Definition:** Age-related loss of power of accommodation.

**Causes:**
- Weakening of ciliary muscles
- Lens becomes less flexible
- Usually after 40 years

**Symptoms:**
- Cannot see near objects clearly
- Difficulty in reading
- Holds book at arm's length

**Correction:**
- **Bifocal lenses**
- Upper part: Concave (distance vision)
- Lower part: Convex (reading vision)

```
BIFOCAL LENS

    ┌─────────────┐
    │             │
    │  Concave    │ ← For distance
    │  (upper)    │
    ├─────────────┤
    │  Convex     │ ← For reading
    │  (lower)    │
    └─────────────┘
```

---

#### 4. Cataract:

**Definition:** Clouding of eye lens causing blurred vision.

**Causes:**
- Age-related
- Injury
- Diabetes
- UV exposure

**Symptoms:**
- Blurred vision
- Faded colors
- Glare
- Poor night vision

**Treatment:**
- Surgery to remove cloudy lens
- Replace with artificial lens
- Spectacles or contact lenses after surgery

### 10.3 Dispersion of Light

#### Dispersion Through Prism:

**Definition:** Splitting of white light into its constituent colors.

```
DISPERSION THROUGH PRISM

    White Light
         │
         │
         ▼
        ╱│╲
       ╱ │ ╲
      ╱  │  ╲
     ╱   │   ╲
    ╱    │    ╲
   ╱     │     ╲
  ╱      │      ╲
 ╱       │       ╲
╱        │        ╲
─────────┴─────────
    V I B G O Y R
    
    Violet deviates MOST
    Red deviates LEAST
    
    Reason: Different wavelengths
    bend by different amounts
```

**Newton's Experiment:**

```
NEWTON'S DISPERSION EXPERIMENT

    Sunlight
       │
       ▼
     Prism 1
       │
       ▼
    Spectrum
    (VIBGYOR)
       │
       ▼
     Prism 2
    (inverted)
       │
       ▼
    White Light
    
    Conclusion: White light contains
    all seven colors
```

**Colors in Order (VIBGYOR):**

1. **V**iolet - 400-450 nm (most deviation)
2. **I**ndigo - 450-500 nm
3. **B**lue - 500-550 nm
4. **G**reen - 550-600 nm
5. **Y**ellow - 600-650 nm
6. **O**range - 650-700 nm
7. **R**ed - 700-750 nm (least deviation)

#### Rainbow Formation:

**Definition:** Natural spectrum formed by dispersion of sunlight by water droplets.

```
RAINBOW FORMATION

         Sun (behind observer)
            │
            │
            │
            ▼
    ┌───────────────┐
    │  Water Drops  │
    │  in Atmosphere│
    └───────┬───────┘
            │
    Refraction + Dispersion
            │
    Internal Reflection
            │
    Refraction again
            │
            ▼
        Observer
        sees rainbow
    
    Primary Rainbow:
    - One internal reflection
    - Red on outer side
    - Violet on inner side
    - Brighter
    
    Secondary Rainbow:
    - Two internal reflections
    - Violet on outer side
    - Red on inner side
    - Fainter
```

**Conditions:**
1. Sun must be behind observer
2. Water droplets in atmosphere
3. Sun low in sky (morning/evening)

### 10.4 Atmospheric Refraction

#### Twinkling of Stars:

**Cause:** Atmospheric refraction of starlight.

```
TWINKLING OF STARS

    Star (point source)
         │
         │
    ─────┼────────── Atmosphere
         │          (varying density)
         │
    ─────┼────────── Observer
         │
         │
    Apparent position
    fluctuates
    
    Effect:
    - Brightness varies
    - Position varies
    - Appears to twinkle
    
    Reason:
    - Point source
    - Light from single point
    - Variations not averaged
```

**Why Planets Don't Twinkle:**
- Extended source (disc-like)
- Light from multiple points
- Variations cancel out
- Steady appearance

---

#### Advanced Sunrise and Delayed Sunset:

**Effect:** Sun visible 2 minutes before actual sunrise and 2 minutes after actual sunset.

```
ADVANCED SUNRISE

    Actual Sun (below horizon)
         │
         │
    ─────┼────────── Horizon
         │
         │  Atmosphere
         │  (denser near surface)
         │
    ─────┼────────── Observer
         │
         │
    Apparent Sun (above horizon)
    
    Effect:
    - Sunrise: 2 minutes earlier
    - Sunset: 2 minutes later
    - Day longer by 4 minutes
    
    Reason: Light bends towards
    normal in denser medium
```

### 10.5 Scattering of Light

#### Tyndall Effect:

**Definition:** Scattering of light by colloidal particles.

**Examples:**
1. **Sunlight through forest:**
   - Dust particles scatter light
   - Visible beam of light

2. **Sunlight through dusty room:**
   - Light path visible
   - Particles scatter light

3. **Blue color of water:**
   - Fine particles scatter blue
   - Appears blue

---

#### Blue Color of Sky:

**Cause:** Scattering of sunlight by atmospheric molecules.

**Explanation:**

```
BLUE SKY

    Sunlight (white)
         │
         │
    ─────┼────────── Atmosphere
         │          (molecules)
         │
    ─────┼────────── Observer
         │
         │
    Blue light scattered
    in all directions
    
    Reason:
    - Blue has shorter wavelength
    - Scatters more (Rayleigh scattering)
    - Reaches eyes from all directions
    - Sky appears blue
```

**Rayleigh Scattering:**
- Scattering ∝ 1/λ⁴
- Shorter wavelength scatters more
- Blue (450 nm) scatters more than red (700 nm)

---

#### Red Color of Sun at Sunrise/Sunset:

**Cause:** More scattering of blue light, red reaches observer.

```
RED SUNRISE/SUNSET

    Sun (low in sky)
         │
         │
         │  Long path through
         │  atmosphere
         │
    ─────┼────────── Atmosphere
         │
         │  Blue scattered away
         │
    ─────┼────────── Observer
         │
         │
    Red light reaches
    (least scattered)
    
    Reason:
    - Sunlight travels longer path
    - Most blue scattered away
    - Red/orange reaches observer
    - Sun appears reddish
```

**At Noon:**
- Sun overhead
- Shorter path
- Less scattering
- Sun appears white

---

#### Danger Signals - Red Color:

**Why Red?**

1. **Least scattered:**
   - Longest wavelength
   - Travels farthest
   - Visible from distance

2. **Penetrates fog/smoke:**
   - Less affected by particles
   - Visible in poor visibility

3. **Attention-grabbing:**
   - Stands out
   - Universal warning color

**Applications:**
- Traffic signals
- Railway signals
- Danger signs
- Tail lights of vehicles

## 4. Guided Examples

### Example 1: Myopia Correction

**Question:** A person cannot see objects beyond 2 m clearly. Calculate the power of lens required to correct this defect.

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

| Step | Action | Working |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Identify the defect | Far point = 2 m (Myopia) |
| 2 | Lens required | Concave lens |
| 3 | Focal length | f = -far point = -2 m |
| 4 | Apply power formula | P = 1/f |
| 5 | Calculate | P = 1/(-2) = -0.5 D |

**Final Answer:** Power = -0.5 D (concave lens)

---

### Example 2: Hypermetropia Correction

**Question:** The near point of a hypermetropic eye is 1 m. Find the power of lens required to correct this defect. (Near point of normal eye = 25 cm)

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

| Step | Action | Working |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Identify given values | Near point = 1 m = 100 cm |
| | | Normal near point = 25 cm |
| 2 | Lens required | Convex lens |
| 3 | Object distance | u = -25 cm |
| 4 | Image distance | v = -100 cm |
| 5 | Apply lens formula | 1/f = 1/v - 1/u |
| 6 | Substitute | 1/f = 1/(-100) - 1/(-25) |
| | | 1/f = -1/100 + 1/25 = (-1+4)/100 = 3/100 |
| 7 | Find f | f = 100/3 cm = 1/3 m |
| 8 | Calculate power | P = 1/f = 1/(1/3) = +3 D |

**Final Answer:** Power = +3 D (convex lens)

---

### Example 3: Dispersion Angle

**Question:** White light is incident on a prism. If violet light deviates by 45° and red light by 40°, calculate the angle of dispersion.

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

| Step | Action | Working |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Identify given values | δᵥ = 45°, δᵣ = 40° |
| 2 | Angle of dispersion | = δᵥ - δᵣ |
| 3 | Calculate | = 45° - 40° = 5° |

**Final Answer:** Angle of dispersion = 5°

---

### Example 4: Presbyopia Correction

**Question:** A person needs a lens of power -5.5 D for distance vision and +1.5 D for near vision. Find the focal lengths.

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

| Step | Action | Working |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | For distance vision | P = -5.5 D |
| 2 | Find focal length | f = 1/P = 1/(-5.5) = -0.182 m = -18.2 cm |
| 3 | For near vision | P = +1.5 D |
| 4 | Find focal length | f = 1/P = 1/1.5 = 0.667 m = 66.7 cm |

**Final Answer:** f₁ = -18.2 cm (concave), f₂ = +66.7 cm (convex)

---

### Example 5: Scattering Comparison

**Question:** If blue light (λ = 450 nm) and red light (λ = 700 nm) are scattered, find the ratio of scattering.

**Step-by-Step Solution:**

| Step | Action | Working |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Rayleigh scattering | Scattering ∝ 1/λ⁴ |
| 2 | Ratio formula | Sᵦ/Sᵣ = (λᵣ/λᵦ)⁴ |
| 3 | Substitute | = (700/450)⁴ |
| 4 | Calculate | = (1.556)⁴ = 5.85 |

**Final Answer:** Blue light scatters 5.85 times more than red light.
