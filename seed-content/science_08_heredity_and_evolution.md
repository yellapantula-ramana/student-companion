# Heredity and Evolution - Learning Path

## 1. Concept Introduction

### What is Heredity?

**Heredity** is the transmission of characters (traits) from parents to offspring. It is the reason why children resemble their parents in various characteristics.

**Everyday Examples:**
- рҹ‘ЁвҖҚрҹ‘©вҖҚрҹ‘§ **Family resemblance:** Eye color, hair type, height
- рҹҗ• **Pet breeding:** Dogs of same breed look similar
- рҹҢҫ **Crop varieties:** High-yield wheat varieties
- рҹҰӢ **Butterfly wings:** Pattern inherited from parents
- рҹҢә **Flower color:** Red flowers produce red-flowered offspring

### What is Evolution?

**Evolution** is the gradual change in living organisms over millions of years, leading to the formation of new species.

**Everyday Examples:**
- рҹҰ• **Fossils:** Dinosaur remains show past life forms
- рҹҗҰ **Bird beaks:** Different shapes for different foods
- рҹҰҹ **Mosquito resistance:** Resistant to pesticides
- рҹҰ  **Bacteria:** Antibiotic resistance
- рҹҗҙ **Horse evolution:** From small to large over time

### 8.1 Heredity - Mendel's Experiments

**Gregor Johann Mendel (1822-1884):** Father of Genetics

**Experiment Plant:** Garden Pea (Pisum sativum)

**Why Pea Plant?**
- Easy to grow
- Short life span
- Self-pollinating
- Many contrasting characters
- Produces many seeds

#### Mendel's Laws of Inheritance:

**1. Law of Dominance:**

```
LAW OF DOMINANCE

    When two different alleles are present:
    - One expresses (Dominant)
    - One remains hidden (Recessive)
    
    Example: Tt plant
    - T (Tall) = Dominant вҶ’ Expressed
    - t (Dwarf) = Recessive вҶ’ Hidden
    - Plant appears Tall
```

**2. Law of Segregation:**

```
LAW OF SEGREGATION

    During gamete formation:
    - Alleles separate
    - Each gamete gets one allele
    - Random combination at fertilization
    
    Tt parent вҶ’ Gametes: T or t (50% each)
```

**3. Law of Independent Assortment:**

```
LAW OF INDEPENDENT ASSORTMENT

    Dihybrid Cross (Two characters)
    
    Parent: Round Yellow (RRYY) Г— Wrinkled Green (rryy)
    
    FвӮҒ: All Round Yellow (RrYy)
    
    FвӮӮ: 
    Round Yellow : Round Green : Wrinkled Yellow : Wrinkled Green
        9         :      3       :        3        :        1
    
    Shows independent inheritance of seed shape and color
```

**(b) Law Derived:**

**Q64.** (a) Explain the experiment that proved DNA is the genetic material.
(b) What is the structure of DNA?

<details>
<summary>вң“ Show Answer</summary>

**Answer:**

**(a) DNA as Genetic Material:**

**Griffith's Experiment (1928):**

**Bacteria:** Streptococcus pneumoniae

**Types:**
- **S-type:** Smooth, virulent, causes pneumonia
- **R-type:** Rough, non-virulent, harmless

**Experiments:**

1. **Live S-type вҶ’ Mouse dies**
2. **Live R-type вҶ’ Mouse lives**
3. **Heat-killed S-type вҶ’ Mouse lives**
4. **Heat-killed S + Live R вҶ’ Mouse dies**
   - Live S-type recovered from dead mouse
   - Transformation occurred

**Conclusion:**
- Something from dead S-type transformed R-type
- "Transforming principle"
- Later proved to be DNA

**Avery, MacLeod, McCarty (1944):**
- Purified transforming principle
- Proved it was DNA
- DNA is genetic material

**(b) DNA Structure:**

**Watson and Crick (1953):**
